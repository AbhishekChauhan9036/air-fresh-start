Air Pollution Awareness

Air pollution is one of the most serious environmental risks to human health. It affects the atmosphere, ecosystems, and quality of life, especially in urban areas.

Major Air Pollutants

  • PM2.5: Fine particles that penetrate deep into the lungs and bloodstream.
  • PM10: Coarse particles affecting respiratory health.
  • NO₂: Causes lung irritation and reduces immunity.
  • SO₂: Leads to breathing difficulty and acid rain.
  • O₃: Triggers asthma and chest pain.

Health Impacts

  • Respiratory diseases (asthma, bronchitis)
  • Cardiovascular problems
  • Reduced lung function in children
  • Premature deaths

Sources of Air Pollution

  • Vehicle emissions
  • Industrial activities
  • Construction dust
  • Biomass burning
  • Power plants

Prevention & Control Measures

  • Use public transport and electric vehicles
  • Adopt renewable energy sources
  • Plant more trees
  • Avoid open burning
  • Use air purifiers indoors

What You Can Do

  • Check daily air quality index (AQI)
  • Wear masks during high pollution
  • Reduce energy consumption
  • Spread awareness in your community
